Understanding Glucose Metrics

HbA1c: A HbA1c test measures a patient's average blood sugar levels over two/three months. This test is often used to diagnose diabetes and the risk of developing diabetes.

Hyperglycemia: Hyperglycemia is a condition where there is too much glucose in your blood. A person is considered to be pre-diabetic if they are in the 140 mg/dL - 200 mg/dL range one/two hours after eating. We chose 170 mg/dL as the threshold for hyperglycemia in our graph because this falls in the middle of that threshold.

Hypoglycemia: Hypoglycemia is a condition where your blood sugar is lower than the normal range, usually considered to be anything below the threshold of 70 mg/dL.

What does it mean to be prediabetic? A patient is considered prediabetic with a HbA1c score of 5.7% - 6.4% and diabetic with a score of > 6.5%.

Prediabetes increases the time someone spends in hyperglycemia compared to someone without prediabetes. Prolonged time spent in hyperglycemia can increase the risk of developing diabetes.
