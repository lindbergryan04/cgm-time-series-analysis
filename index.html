Patient Glucose Monitoring

Data Context

About the Data: The glucose and dietary data in this visualization comes from a 10-day wearable device study conducted by the BIG IDEAs Lab at Duke University. Participants wore Dexcom G6 CGMs and Empatica E4 wristbands while logging their meals. You can read more about the dataset here.

HbA1c: The HbA1c test measures a patient’s average blood sugar levels over the past two to three months. It’s commonly used to diagnose diabetes and assess the risk of developing it.

Hyperglycemia: Hyperglycemia refers to having too much glucose in the blood. A person is considered prediabetic if their levels fall between 140–200 mg/dL one to two hours after eating. We chose 170 mg/dL as the hyperglycemia threshold in our graph, as it sits near the midpoint of this range.

Hypoglycemia: Hypoglycemia occurs when blood sugar drops below normal levels — typically anything under 70 mg/dL.

Prediabetes: A person is considered prediabetic if their HbA1c is between 5.7% and 6.4%. A score of 6.5% or higher is considered diabetic. People with prediabetes tend to spend more time in a hyperglycemic state compared to those without it. Extended time in this range increases the risk of developing type 2 diabetes and other complications.
